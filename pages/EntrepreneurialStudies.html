Entrepreneurial Studies
Credits:
2 semesters, 1 credit per semester

Prerequisites:
None.

This interdisciplinary, yearlong elective course introduces students to the basic principles of business with a focus on entrepreneurship. Students will gain at least a rudimentary understanding of economics, business law (types of corporations), personal finance and investment, business structure and roles, business plan finance and accounting, market research and analysis, and entrepreneurship. They will constantly apply their new learning in these areas to the design of their own business plan. Students will also hear from guest speakers, including entrepreneurs. (from the course catalog)
